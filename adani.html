Stage 0: Maturity Assessment & Target State

Baseline challenges and define the entry stage (target state) before execution
0

Key challenges (current state)

Disintegrated plants, decentralized processes, heavy Excel reporting, manual KPI calculations, and limited margin/EBITDA visibility.

Target state (entry stage)

Start with Stage 1: unify data foundation across plants and functions to enable trusted, scalable reporting and control.

Outcome of Stage 0: agreed scope, sequencing, owners, and success metrics so the organization knows where to start and what “done” means.

ROI / Business value by stage
Delivered impact (Stages 1–2) + roadmap value (Stages 3–5)
Executive impact summary
  • Speed: 6-week time-to-value to establish an enterprise control layer
  • Efficiency: ~$1M realized labor & infrastructure savings with reporting automation
  • Control: Standardized KPIs (SACC, NSP, EBITDA) to reduce metric disputes and drive action-oriented reviews
  • Roadmap focus: Predictability, cost optimization, and controlled autonomy (exception-based management)

Stage 1 ROI

  • Created a single source of truth across plants, reducing reconciliation cycles and reporting friction
  • Improved decision confidence through centralized controls, access governance, and auditability
  • Enabled scalable rollout of new dashboards and use cases without duplicating infrastructure per plant
(Delivered)

Stage 2 ROI

  • Compressed reporting cycle time by ~95%, enabling faster performance correction
  • Reduced manual intervention by ~30%, freeing operational bandwidth for higher-value work
  • Delivered ~$1M labor & infrastructure efficiencies through automation and standardization
  • Shifted leadership reviews from data reconciliation to performance action
(Delivered)

Stage 3 ROI

  • Earlier demand and supply risk signals to reduce planning surprises and protect revenue and margin
  • Scenario-based S&OP decisions with explainable drivers (why / what-if) to improve alignment and speed
  • Strengthened planning discipline via forecast tracking, override governance, and drift monitoring
(Roadmap)

Stage 4 ROI

  • Direct cost reduction by optimizing logistics routes, inventory positioning, and constraint-aware plans
  • Maximize contribution margin per ton by allocating constrained capacity to the most profitable segments.
  • Faster execution by embedding optimization recommendations directly into operational workflows
(Roadmap)

Stage 5 ROI

  • Shift to exception-based management to scale execution without linear headcount growth
  • Safer automation of low-risk decisions using clear guardrails, escalation paths, and human oversight
  • Lower enterprise risk exposure through traceability, audit logs, and continuous outcome monitoring
(Roadmap)
