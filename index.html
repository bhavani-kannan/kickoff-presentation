Stage 1: Data Foundation

Trusted, governed data assets
1
  • Define data domains, ownership, accountability
  • Integrate priority systems into one architecture
  • Standardize data models and master data alignment
  • Set quality standards, monitoring, controls
  • Establish security and access foundation

Stage 2: Standardized Intelligence

Consistent performance view
2
  • Define standarized KPI library (EBITDA, CoP, PTPK, etc.) and metric governance
  • Implement shared semantic layer with approved metric formulas
  • Enable governed self-service analytics on trusted KPIs
  • Set reporting lifecycle, data standards and metric controls
  • Track adoption and KPI usage across business units

Stage 3: Predictive Planning

Foresight built into planning
3
  • Prioritize predictive use cases aligned to strategic KPIs
  • Develop and validate forecasting models for key metrics
  • Integrate KPI forecasts into planning and operations
  • Establish model lifecycle oversight and performance review
  • Monitor forecast accuracy and impact vs actual results

Stage 4: Prescriptive Decisions

Optimization and decision automation
4
  • Select high-value decisions for optimization
  • Embed business constraints and policy rules
  • Deploy prescriptive engines (optimization, simulation)
  • Integrate recommendations into workflows
  • Measure realized savings and margin lift

Stage 5: Controlled Autonomy

Execute with guardrails
5
  • Define autonomy boundaries and escalation paths
  • Implement human-in-the-loop oversight
  • Automate actions for low-risk decisions
  • Continuously monitor drift, bias, and compliance
  • Scale autonomy across functions responsibly

ROI / Business value by stage
Executive outcomes expressed in business impact

Stage 1 ROI

  • Reduce reporting effort and cycle time through trusted data availability
  • Lower risk exposure via standardized controls, access, and auditability
  • Faster time-to-value for every downstream use case

Stage 2 ROI

  • Faster decisions through a single “certified KPI” view of performance
  • Reduce metric disputes and rework; improve accountability
  • Self-service analytics at scale to lower BI/ops dependency cost

Stage 3 ROI

  • Improve forecast accuracy - better S&OP, fewer surprises
  • Working capital benefits via inventory and capacity alignment
  • Revenue and margin protection through early risk detection

Stage 4 ROI

  • Direct cost reduction through optimization (logistics, procurement, ops)
  • Margin lift from better allocation, mix, and constraint-aware decisions
  • Faster cycle time from decision recommendations embedded in workflows

Stage 5 ROI

  • Exception-based management to reduced operational overhead
  • Higher throughput and responsiveness via controlled automation
  • Lower risk with guardrails, traceability, and human oversight
